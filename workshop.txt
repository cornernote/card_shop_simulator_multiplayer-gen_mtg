[h1]GenMTG[/h1]

Ever wanted [b]Card Shop Simulator Multiplayer[/b] to feel like cracking open an old-school [b]Magic: The Gathering[/b] binder?

[b]GenMTG[/b] replaces the full vanilla collectible card pool with classic MTG cards from [b]Old School 93/94[/b] and the [b]Pre-Modern[/b] era. Expect iconic creatures, strange early-Magic oddities, legendary chase cards, and enough flavor matching that the original card slots still make sense.

[h2]=== FEATURES ===[/h2]

[list]
[*][b]449 card replacements[/b] - every collectible card currently found in the game inventory is mapped to an MTG card.
[*][b]Full pool coverage[/b] - includes gens 1-7, special packs, holiday/event cards, divine/god cards, souvenir cards, and commemorative cards.
[*][b]Classic MTG icons[/b] - includes cards like [b]Shivan Dragon[/b], [b]Black Lotus[/b], [b]Juzam Djinn[/b], [b]Serra Angel[/b], [b]Sol Ring[/b], [b]Nicol Bolas[/b], [b]Karn, Silver Golem[/b], and [b]Akroma, Angel of Wrath[/b].
[*][b]Vanilla game flow preserved[/b] - keeps the original pack structure, rarity bands, card values, and collection progression.
[*][b]Creature-first mapping[/b] - most normal card slots become classic MTG creatures.
[*][b]Spell-flavored slots[/b] - selected spell-like cards use iconic spells, enchantments, and artifacts.
[*][b]No MTG lands[/b] - lands are intentionally excluded because they do not fit the creature-card style of the game.
[*][b]Custom flavor blurbs[/b] - card descriptions use short custom flavor text instead of full MTG rules text.
[/list]

[h2]=== HOW IT WORKS ===[/h2]

The mod changes the visible [b]card name[/b], [b]description[/b], [b]artwork[/b], and [b]big-card image mode[/b] while preserving the original game data for rarity, value, and pack placement.

That means chase cards still behave like chase cards, event cards still come from their event sources, and the game still plays like [b]Card Shop Simulator Multiplayer[/b] - just with a lot more Magic on the cards.

[h2]=== MTG ERA ===[/h2]

The card pool focuses on:

[list]
[*][b]Old School 93/94[/b]
[*][b]Pre-Modern-era Magic[/b]
[/list]

The goal is classic flavor over perfect tournament legality.
